Latin at College

The Latin language provides academic challenge and the study of the Romans provides intrinsically interesting literature and background. College uses the long-standing and highly accessible Cambridge Latin Course.

Work combines grammatical exercises, unseen translation and comprehension, and cultural research and presentation. The Third Form course offers the opportunity for all pupils to access GCSE study, regardless of how much Latin you have done before coming to College.

Classical Greek Taster

In the Spring Term, all those studying Latin are given a taster opportunity to study Classical Greek. Taught by specialists, this allows you to access the richly rewarding study of not only a different alphabet from our own but also another rewarding Classical language, which can also be studied at GCSE.

Following John Taylor's Greek to GCSE course, lessons use a similar structure to Latin.

Perfect for [Child's Name]

What does the course cover?

Those not studying Latin study a wide range of aspects of the ancient world in English translation. Ever-popular myths and legends from the Greeks and Romans are studied alongside their lives and works, with a particular focus on what it actually meant to be "Greek" or "Roman".

Our exciting in-house course also looks further afield, to the civilisations of Persia and Egypt, to look at how the Classical world dealt with outsiders and reflect on what we can learn about modern-day attitudes from our ancestors.

Accessibility and Pathways

While enjoyment and success in Third Form is a good foundation for studying Classical Civilisation at GCSE, the subject remains highly accessible for those who have studied Latin in Third Form to begin from scratch.

Course Content

  • Greek and Roman myths and legends
  • Ancient lives and cultural works
  • Persian and Egyptian civilizations
  • Classical world interactions

Modern Relevance

  • Contemporary attitude reflection
  • Cultural identity exploration
  • Historical perspective development
  • Cross-cultural understanding

Classical Tradition at College

The Classics Department comprises six specialist teachers and six classrooms situated in a historic part of the central Quad. A rotating programme of annual lectures, competitions and trips is complemented by the lively pupil-led Classics Society, featuring games, quizzes and talks by pupils and staff at twice-termly evening events.

EAL at College

If English is not your native language and you are deemed to need support, you will have English as an Additional Language lessons when your peers are learning another language, normally French. In the Third Form, you have three lessons per week together as a class.

EAL lessons aim to equip you with the necessary skills and confidence in the use of English language to allow you to integrate successfully in the mainstream curriculum and achieve your full potential at College.

Teaching Approach

Lower College EAL pupils enjoy the interactive and engaging approach adopted in lessons, aiming to develop your language to a high enough academic standard to cope with the demanding British curriculum, while supporting and consolidating your skills.

The detail of lessons depends on any group's particular needs, as identified through diagnostic testing. However, the focus of the lessons will be on enhancing all four skills: listening, reading, writing and speaking.

Core Skills Development

  • Vocabulary building programmes
  • Grammatical accuracy strengthening
  • Communicative approach
  • Integrated skill development

Cross-Curricular Support

  • "Learning to learn" focus

Individual Support & Enrichment

Individual one-to-one lessons, for which there is an extra charge, are also available if there is need for further support. Those identified with particular needs are encouraged to take extra lessons and your parents/guardians will be informed.

We are keen to showcase the talents of our pupils and enter you for competitions where possible. Recent competitions have included the Elmbridge short story competition, the Foyle Young Poets of the Year competition and the Oxford Science and Ideas Festival — Poetry of Science competition, for which one of our pupils gained the top prize.

Language Learning & Cultural Understanding

Two-Language Approach: All Third Form pupils study two languages - one as a continuation from previous studies and one as a beginner language. This broad approach ensures exposure to different linguistic structures and cultural perspectives.

Cultural Enrichment: Language learning at College goes beyond grammar and vocabulary to include deep cultural understanding. Students explore history, traditions, and contemporary issues in French, Spanish, German, and classical cultures.

International Perspective: With EAL support for international students and classical studies connecting to ancient civilizations, the language programme develops true global awareness and cross-cultural communication skills essential for modern citizenship.

GCSE Preparation: All language courses provide solid grammatical foundations and cultural knowledge essential for successful GCSE study, with regular trips abroad and exchange programmes enhancing practical language use.
